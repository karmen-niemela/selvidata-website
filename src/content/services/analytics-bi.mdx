## Project examples

### B2B SaaS — unifying product and sales data

A typical starting point: product usage events in Mixpanel, customer and deal data in Salesforce, and subscription data in Stripe — with no way to connect them. The sales team has no visibility into how prospects are using the trial product, and the product team can't see which features correlate with expansion revenue.

The project would involve building a unified data model in a warehouse that joins product events to accounts, linked to deal stage and ARR. The outcome is a single view of the customer: which features drive conversion from trial to paid, which usage patterns predict churn, and where high-value accounts are under-utilizing the product.

### B2B SaaS — first product instrumentation and cohort analysis

A common early-stage situation: signup numbers come from the auth system, but there's no insight into what happens after — activation rates, feature adoption, or where users drop off in onboarding.

This type of project starts with designing an event tracking schema and wiring it up across the product, then building cohort-based dashboards that break down signup-to-activation rates by acquisition channel, plan type, or product version. The goal is to give the product and growth teams a reliable foundation for understanding what's actually working.
